In a journey to expand human knowledge, we struggle to extract meaningful information from the torrent of the data that our world is flooded with. Once upon a time, the clever plotting method was the only thing needed to solve the cholera problem and prevent countless life losses (I guess that John Snow *did* know something). Even though less dramatic, the impact of visual representation of the data is still pretty considerable, since even if the analysis is done by fancy statistical methods and deep learning techniques, the decisions are usually made according to easy-to-digest plots and one-number summaries. This post is my list of plots that I reach for the most. Plots that in my opinion and for my purposes were able to effectively tell a story. Simple enough that can be understood by the non-technical audience (managers and salespeople) and beautiful enough to be presented to clients.

The Iris almighty

For example, we can plot ALL information from Fisher's famous Iris data set on one plot (I know, Iris *again*, but bear with me. That's the one we are all familiar with) by fusion between a bubble and scatter plot.
